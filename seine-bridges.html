Seine Bridges in Paris: the Iconic Crossings You Shouldn’t Miss

Paris is stitched together by its bridges. Some are centuries old, some are ornate masterpieces, and others are pure postcard viewpoints. If you only have time for a handful, this guide helps you choose.

🌉 See the Bridges

How many bridges cross the Seine in Paris?

In central Paris, the Seine is crossed by dozens of bridges that connect the Left Bank and Right Bank and link the historic islands. You don’t need to see them all — you just need the right mix of history, views, and location.

Below are the most famous bridges visitors search for (and the ones you’ll recognize instantly from photos).

The Most Iconic Seine Bridges

These picks lean toward bridges that combine real historic weight with what travelers actually want: great photos, easy access, and “wow” views.

Pont Neuf

The “New Bridge” that’s actually Paris’ oldest surviving bridge.

  • Why it matters: a turning point in how Paris connected its islands to the city.
  • Best for: classic Seine views + easy access to Île de la Cité.

Pont Alexandre III

The most ornate bridge in Paris — statues, lamps, and golden details.

  • Why it matters: Belle Époque Paris at its most theatrical.
  • Best for: golden-hour photos and evening walks.

Pont des Arts

A pedestrian bridge between the Louvre area and Saint‑Germain.

  • Why it matters: one of the best “stop and linger” viewpoints over the river.
  • Best for: sunsets, street musicians, and relaxed people‑watching.

Pont de Bir‑Hakeim

The iron bridge with the cinematic structure (and killer Eiffel Tower angles).

  • Why it matters: industrial-era Paris meets the postcard skyline.
  • Best for: Eiffel Tower photos without the crowds of the Champs de Mars.

Pont Marie

A quieter historic bridge near Île Saint‑Louis.

  • Why it matters: a calmer slice of old Paris away from the busiest sights.
  • Best for: combining with a stroll on Île Saint‑Louis.

Pont du Carrousel

A sleek bridge with Louvre and riverbank views.

  • Why it matters: one of the easiest places to connect the museum core to the river.
  • Best for: pairing the Louvre with a Seine walk or cruise.
Tip: If you want bridges + monuments with minimal planning, a Seine sightseeing cruise is the easiest way to see a lot in one hour.

A simple “bridges walk” you can do in 90 minutes

Want the bridges without overthinking? Start near the Louvre and let the river guide you.

  1. Pont des Arts — stop for the classic river panorama.
  2. Pont Neuf — cross toward Île de la Cité for the historic heart of Paris.
  3. Continue along the quays — you’ll pass perfect viewpoints back toward the Louvre and the Left Bank.

Prefer to end with something unforgettable? Pair the walk with a Seine dinner cruise the same evening.
