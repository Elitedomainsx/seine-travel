Why Dine on the Seine?

An evening cruise along the Seine isn’t just another meal — it’s a front‑row seat to Paris at its most enchanting. As you drift past illuminated monuments like the Eiffel Tower and Notre‑Dame, a multi‑course dinner is served at your table. The ever‑changing views and romantic glow of the city lights have made these sailings one of Paris’s quintessential experiences.

From gourmet indulgences accompanied by live music to relaxed bistro‑style outings, there’s a dinner cruise for every taste and budget. The table below offers a quick comparison to help you choose the evening that suits your mood and your itinerary.

Dinner Cruise Reviews & Insider Notes

Bateaux Parisiens – Dinner Cruise With Live Music

Departing from the foot of the Eiffel Tower, Bateaux Parisiens marries haute cuisine with a glass‑roofed vessel that affords panoramic views. The three‑course à la carte menu features seasonally inspired dishes such as foie gras, fresh fish and decadent desserts. Optional upgrades — from Champagne and cheese courses to guaranteed window seats — allow you to tailor the experience. Live music sets the mood as you float past Les Invalides, the Musée d’Orsay and Notre‑Dame.

This flagship cruise sits at the higher end of the price scale, but its romance factor makes it a favourite for anniversaries and proposals. Seating in the centre of the boat can feel a little tight, so it’s worth paying extra for a bow‑side table if you’re celebrating something special.

Perfect for couples and anyone who wants to savour Paris at a leisurely pace, with all the trimmings of a special occasion.

Eiffel Croisières – Bistronomic Dinner Cruise

Operated by the family‑run Eiffel Croisières, this relaxed 1½‑ to 2‑hour dinner boards near the Pont Alexandre III. Instead of high‑end gastronomy, expect a bistronomic three‑course meal featuring seasonal soups, pâtés, comforting meat or fish mains and a classic dessert. Staff are attentive and the boat’s open‑air upper deck invites you to capture the Eiffel Tower as it sparkles on the hour.

Drinks aren’t included by default, though you can order wine or cocktails on board and arrange extras like guaranteed window seats or celebration cakes. With its smaller size and convivial atmosphere, this cruise offers great value for couples, groups and families who prefer a laid‑back evening on the water. Note that the boat isn’t wheelchair accessible and seats fill up quickly, so reservations are essential.

Ideal if you’re seeking a romantic evening on a budget or travelling with friends who enjoy friendly service and simple French fare.

Compagnie des Bateaux Mouches – 4‑Course Dinner Cruise

If you’re looking for grand theatre on the water, the Bateaux Mouches four‑course dinner cruise delivers. Boarding near Pont de l’Alma, you’re greeted with red‑carpet formality before being escorted to your table. A set menu unfolds with amuse‑bouche and starters such as foie gras or shrimp tartare, indulgent mains like filet of beef or roasted fish, followed by cheese and dessert. An apéritif, half bottle of wine or Champagne and coffee are included.

Live piano and violin lend an old‑world charm as the boat glides past the Eiffel Tower, Louvre and Notre‑Dame. Dress smartly — shorts and flip‑flops are discouraged — and be prepared to pay a premium for this romantic show. Service can feel brisk on busy nights, but the atmosphere makes it a favourite for proposals and milestone celebrations.

Good for those seeking a lavish Parisian night with live music and top‑notch cuisine.

Paris en Scène – Panoramic Dinner Cruise

For travellers short on time, Paris en Scène offers a 105‑minute dinner cruise departing from Île aux Cygnes. Cosy armchairs face expansive windows, and a terrace allows 360° photos as you sail past the Louvre, Musée d’Orsay and Eiffel Tower. The three‑course cocotte menu is simple but hearty — think beef bourguignon, salmon with lentils and classic French desserts. Water is included; wine, soft drinks and coffee cost extra.

The relaxed lounge vibe and clever seating layout make this one of the most affordable ways to dine on the Seine. It’s especially popular with families, photographers and visitors who want a taste of river life without committing to a longer, more formal evening. Foodies may find the menu straightforward, but the views and price point are hard to beat.

Ideal for travellers on a tighter schedule or budget, families with kids and anyone chasing those Eiffel Tower shots.

Paris City Vision – Gourmet Choice Cruise

Operated by Paris City Vision offers two departure times: an early 75‑minute sail and a two‑hour evening voyage from a quay near the Musée d’Orsay. The intimate glass‑roofed boat serves a three‑course dinner where you choose each course; coffee is included and upgrades allow you to add Champagne or wine.

Menus might feature seared scallops or foie gras starters, tournedos of beef or fish mains, and a range of desserts, with vegetarian options available. Guests appreciate the high quality of the food and the ability to tailor their meal. The vibe is casually elegant, so there’s no strict dress code. Note that ordering steak incurs a small extra charge and drinks beyond coffee aren’t included.

Perfect for foodies and couples who value flexibility — the early sailing is ideal if you’ve got evening plans elsewhere.

Planning Tips & FAQs

Plan ahead

Dinner cruises sell out quickly, especially on summer weekends. To secure your preferred time and seating it’s wise to reserve a few days — or even weeks — in advance.

What to wear & dietary needs

Most operators encourage smart casual attire: men usually opt for slacks or neat jeans with a collared shirt, while women choose a dress or elegant trousers. Leave shorts and flip‑flops at home, and bring a light jacket for breezy evenings on deck. Vegetarian dishes are always available, and vegan or gluten‑free meals can be prepared if you mention your requirements when booking.

Cancellation & weather

Tickets booked through GetYourGuide typically come with free cancellation up to 24 hours before departure, and many allow you to reserve now and pay later. Cruises run rain or shine in climate‑controlled dining rooms; in the rare event of severe weather or high water, operators will offer a reschedule or refund.
